1 · Safety & essentials

TIVA — safe conduct (AAGBI/SIVA 2019)

Equipment

  • TCI pump with correct drug / concentration / algorithm / patient details (2-person check).

  • Anti-reflux valve on fluid; anti-syphon valves on drug lines.

  • Luer-lock syringes; lines joined close to patient; avoid 3-way taps on drug line.

  • 1% propofol in general theatres; 2% in neuro/ICU.

  • Remifentanil TCI diluted to 50 mcg/ml.

  • Cannula secure, visible, not antecubital fossa if avoidable.

Marsh plasma targets (healthy adult)

  • Induction: propofol 4–6 mcg/ml

  • Maintenance (no opioid): 3–6 mcg/ml

  • Maintenance (with opioid): 2.5–4 mcg/ml

  • Older / frail: lower targets

Non-negotiables

  • Depth-of-anaesthesia monitor whenever NMB used.

  • Do not mix opioid with propofol in one syringe.

  • Flush line after use with >2× dead-space.

  • Obesity: Adjusted Body Weight; depth monitor essential.

  • Paediatric TIVA only if specifically trained.

Reference: AAGBI/SIVA. Anaesthesia 2019, 74, 211–224. BSUH TIVA single sheet v1.1, Feb 2019.

Depth-of-anaesthesia monitor setup (BSUH)

Entropy (GE monitor)

  • Monitor setup → Screen setup → tick Entropy.

  • Entropy box → Scale 50 µV (25 µV in elderly), sweep 12.5 mm/s, Display All.

  • Burst Suppression Ratio should generally be 0%.

BIS

  • Interpret raw waveform with filters off.

  • Target 40–60 for GA; rising SR warns of over-dosage.

Rib-fracture pathway (BSUH chest trauma)

  • 10–15% of blunt trauma patients fracture ribs.

  • Risk factors for poor outcome: >3 ribs, age >65, chest infection.

  • Use the Rib Fracture Score in ED → escalate to HDU/ICU if high.

  • Early regional: serratus anterior catheter, paravertebral, thoracic epidural.

  • Chest physio, humidified O₂, suction, mobilisation.

Local anaesthetic systemic toxicity — drill

Progressive signs

  • Peri-oral tingling, light-headedness, tremor, tinnitus, blurred vision.

  • Twitching → LOC, seizures → CVS collapse/arrest.

Management

  • Stop injection. ABC, 100% O₂, call for help.

  • Intralipid 20%: 1.5 ml/kg bolus then 15 ml/kg/h; max 10 ml/kg first 30 min.

  • Benzodiazepine or thiopentone for seizures; avoid propofol if CV unstable.

  • Prolonged CPR may be required (>1 h). Transfer to ICU.

Subarachnoid injection from an eye block causes an immediate total spinal — intubate, ventilate, support CVS.

2 · Obstetrics

Elective LSCS — spinal (NALHN recipe)

  • Mix: 2.2–2.4 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine + 15 mcg (0.3 ml) fentanyl + 100 mcg (0.2 ml) PF morphine

  • Volume: 2.2 ml if short stature; 2.4 ml standard

  • Pre-load: co-load 500–1000 ml crystalloid at induction

  • Vasopressor: metaraminol attached (prophylactic infusion or boluses); phenylephrine where available

  • Uterotonic: carbetocin 100 mcg — slow, 2 separate pushes post-delivery, keep syringe separate

  • Analgesia: paracoxib IV; NO ibuprofen × 12 h (chart on Sunrise)

  • EBL: generally <1.5 L acceptable — discuss with surgeon

  • Rescue: GTN standby; IV fentanyl for breakthrough — always offer GA

Draw fentanyl + morphine into one syringe technique — fewer syringes, cleaner field.

Labour epidural — loading (NALHN)

Pre-siting

  • Check indication, Hb, platelets, coags.

  • Consent (written preferred, verbal acceptable).

  • Skin local: 5 ml in holding, 5 ml in theatre, 5 ml on bed.

Loading (0.2% ropivacaine)

  • Test dose 6 ml before securing catheter.

  • 1 ml post-fix to check patency.

  • 5 min → check no motor block/BP → 5 ml.

  • Another 5 min → 5 ml.

  • Total loading 14–20 ml of 0.2% ropivacaine.

  • Aim 45 min skin-infiltration to comfort.

  • Do not start PCEA until 30 min after loading; can give IV fentanyl 50–100 mcg to bridge.

  • Non-NALHN difficult positioning: mini-spinal 0.5–1 ml 0.25% bupivacaine or 3 ml bag solution.

Maintenance (once comfortable)

  • PIEB with ropivacaine 0.1% + fentanyl 2 mcg/ml (motor-sparing — preferred over lignocaine for troubleshooting a working labour epidural).

  • Local audit benchmarks: 30 min request-to-attendance; 45 min skin-infiltration-to-comfortable.

Reference: NALHN Obstetric Anaesthetic Service guideline / SSI05511 (local, v2/26).

Risk-stratify every labour epidural for possible CS

  • ~35–40% of primigravidae with a labour epidural proceed to CS (LMH audit).

  • Higher-risk cohorts: VBAC, morbid obesity (BMI >40), twins, severe pre-eclampsia.

  • Round on epidurals during the shift and at handover — identify the partially-working catheter before the Cat 1.

  • An epidural that is patchy or one-sided in labour will likely fail at CS. Replace (or convert to CSE) early rather than flogging it under time pressure.

  • Obstetric pain pathways: 1st stage T10–L1 (visceral); 2nd stage / perineal S2–S4 (somatic) — plan block height accordingly.

Reference: ESAIC focused guidelines on managing the failing labour epidural (Brogly et al., Eur J Anaesthesiol 2025).

Epidural top-up for LSCS

Setup

  • Don't press bolus before top-up; wait 15 min from last bolus.

  • Top up in theatre (not room); test with 2 ml syringe.

  • Allow 15 min from top-up to knife-to-skin.

Recipe A (preferred)

  • 15 ml 0.75% ropivacaine + 2 ml fentanyl (100 mcg) = 17 ml.

  • 3 ml test → 5 ml reassess → 5 ml reassess.

  • Add 2.5 mg diamorphine epidural at end if no intrathecal opioid.

Recipe B

  • 10 ml 2% lidocaine + 10 ml 0.5% bupivacaine.

  • Re-top 3 ml at a time after 45 min if needed.

Drug-choice pearls

  • 2% lidocaine + adrenaline — faster onset, preferred when urgency is high. Shorter duration — watch if surgery >60 min.

  • Ropivacaine 0.75% — slower onset, longer block. Chlorprocaine is not available in Australia.

  • Pre-mix the chosen solution, label it, and keep the syringe attached to the epidural filter to minimise wrong-route errors (a near-miss for carbetocin/TXA/antibiotic). Unsupervised mixing of lidocaine/bicarb/adrenaline by junior trainees is discouraged.

  • Epidural fentanyl 50–100 mcg IT/epidural as adjuvant blunts visceral/vagal discomfort at peritoneal traction.

Block testing before knife-to-skin

  • Use two modalities if in any doubt (cold + pinprick/blunt cannula; light touch + cold).

  • OAA target: loss of light touch to T5, cold (ice) to T4, extending to sacral dermatomes.

  • Must demonstrate loss of hip flexion — if the mother can straight-leg-raise, the block is unlikely to be adequate (ankle movement does not exclude sacral sparing).

  • Interpreting dermatome asymmetry:

    • ≤4 levels difference — lie low side down, give further bolus, reassess.
    • ≥5 levels difference — positioning unlikely to rescue; consider alternative and call consultant early.
    • Block not rising after 15 ml — call consultant; GA may be safer than spinal.

  • Do not perform a spinal after a failed epidural top-up without consultant present — high spinal risk persists even with reduced dose, especially if topped up in the last 30 min. CSE is a safer extension strategy.

Decision-to-delivery context

  • Warren et al. (Int J Obstet Anesth 2018;44:46–52): median decision-to-delivery 14 min for GA vs 16 min for epidural top-up at a tertiary centre — a working catheter is usually quick enough; a patchy one is not.

LA toxicity ceilings

  • Ropivacaine ~3 mg/kg; lignocaine with adrenaline ~7 mg/kg — stop and count if multiple top-ups.

Hypotension on top-up

  • Less common than after a spinal; if persistent or escalating vasopressor needs, think concealed blood loss before anything else. Escalate at EBL >1,500 ml.
References: OAA 2022 (Platt F, Stanford SER, Lucas DN. Prevention and management of intra-operative pain during CS
under neuraxial anaesthesia. Anaesthesia 2022;77:588–597); ASA 2023 Statement on Pain During Caesarean Delivery;
SOAP Peripartum Pain Management Toolkit 2025; Radwan et al. Total spinal after obstetric neuraxial block, IJOA 2024.
If pain persists: offer GA and document. Remove a poorly functioning catheter rather than flogging it.

CSE for LSCS

  • Needle-through-needle or 2-level (epidural + spinal).

  • Don't place on the toilet seat — blocks access.

  • Top up epi at 60–90 min in 5 ml increments (2% lidocaine + adrenaline).

  • Intrathecal fentanyl 15 mcg acceptable adjunct.

Epidural troubleshooting

  • Check: machine → catheter → patient and block level.

  • Sudden stop — suspect migration; re-site.

  • One-sided — lie affected side down.

  • Not pressing PCEA — educate.

  • Breakthrough: 5 ml 0.25% bupivacaine or 5 ml 2% lidocaine with flush; no button press after.

  • 2nd stage / imminent delivery — may not need further intervention.

Intra-uterine resuscitation

  • Left lateral tilt; high-flow O₂ mask.

  • Tocolysis: GTN spray 400–800 mcg SL or IV GTN 50–100 mcg bolus.

  • Crystalloid bolus; stop oxytocin.

  • Call obstetrician; prepare theatre if no recovery.

Cervical suture (cerclage)

GA

  • 12–16 weeks, no aspiration risk → 2nd-gen LMA acceptable.

  • Propofol, fentanyl, ondansetron, paracetamol, dex — keep drugs clean.

Spinal

  • Low dose: 2.2 ml 0.5% heavy bupivacaine + 15 mcg fentanyl.

  • Sit up 30–60 s; slight head-up; target block ≈ T10.

  • Check sacral block (feet) — can be sacral-sparing.

Trial of instrumental delivery in theatre

  • Confirm with obstetrician: position/station may have changed since BAU. Rotational Kielland forceps now rarely used at LMH (3rd/4th- degree tear risk).

  • A dense perineal block is the goal, and is sometimes hard to achieve.

  • Minimum 5 ml 2% lidocaine + adrenaline via the epidural; add fentanyl 50–100 mcg to improve quality.

  • Keep the oxytocin ward line running (or alert the obstetrician if it has been stopped).

  • If instrumental delivery fails → this is a Cat 1 LSCS — rapidly top up with the pre-labelled syringe.

Difficult lumbar neuraxial — paramedian/paraspinous approach

Indications

  • Cannot flex the back (pain, obesity, marked lordosis, arthritis).

  • Kyphoscoliosis or previous lumbar surgery.

  • Non-obstetric: calcified interspinous ligament; thoracic epidurals T3–T7.

Technique

  • First resistance felt is often the ligamentum flavum (feel is different from midline).

  • Walk off the superior margin of the lamina — caution, hitting bone with a spinal needle can blunt/bend the tip.

  • Keep the needle straight when advancing or redirecting.

  • More caudal approach: skin entry 1–1.5 cm lateral to midline, 1 cm caudad to tip of inferior spinous process; cephalad angulation up to 45°.

  • Less caudal approach: 1–1.5 cm lateral, 0.5 cm caudad; less cephalad angulation required.

  • Ultrasound pre-procedural scanning helps identify midline, interspace and depth in difficult anatomy.

Reference: Poots C & Chin KJ. Strategies for successful lumbar neuraxial anaesthesia and analgesia in patients with
challenging anatomy. BJA Education 2024;24:46–56. (Diagrams from Cousins & Bridenbaugh’s Neural Blockade.)

GA for caesarean — core points

  • Pre-oxygenate 3 min / ETO₂ >0.85; ramped position; aspiration precautions.

  • OG tube + suction before extubation.

  • Consider awake fibre-optic if predicted difficulty.

  • Draw up GA drugs before starting section list.

  • Opioid post-delivery: fentanyl / alfentanil ± morphine.

Consultant-escalation triggers (obstetric)

  • Cat 1 LSCS; any GA for an obstetric patient.

  • Placenta praevia/abruption, uterine scar dehiscence, severe pre-eclampsia, multiple pregnancy, BMI >50, prematurity (<32 wk).

  • Haemorrhage >1,500 ml with ongoing loss.

  • Failed epidural extension / intra-operative pain not settled by simple measures / accidental dural puncture.

  • Difficulty siting a spinal or epidural in Delivery Suite or theatre.

  • Obstetric Code Blue, eclampsia, collapsed/unconscious patient.

Postnatal red-flag review

  • Escalate to the Birthing Registrar (and consultant as required) if:

    • New or postural headache after neuraxial; severe headache; tinnitus or muffled hearing (PDPH pathway).

    • Persistent sensory or motor block; unable to straight-leg-raise or move either foot 4 h after delivery.

    • New back pain, pain/swelling/inflammation at the insertion site.

    • New tingling, numbness or paraesthesiae in either/both legs.

    • New urinary or faecal incontinence.

  • Document every neuraxial follow-up on the Obstetric Anaesthesia Audit Form (LSCS and labouring PCEA) and on EMR (.obsaps / .obstele acronyms at NALHN).

Mid-trimester IOL / GTOPs and labour PCA options

  • Fentanyl PCA for mid-trimester inductions / GTOPs where the fetus is not expected to survive.

  • Remifentanil PCA for labour analgesia — discuss with consultant first; follow the local intra-department guideline. 1:1 nursing and continuous SpO₂/capnography monitoring essential.

3 · Orthopaedics

#NOF / Hemi / DHS / TFNA / Gamma nail — 'Tom Fox' technique

  • Airway: GA LMA/ETT ± art line

  • Blocks: Fascia Iliaca + LCFN (hemi/NOF); PENG + LCFN for THR

  • Spinal option: 3 ml 0.5% plain bupivacaine + 20 mcg fentanyl — lateral bad-side-up

  • Asleep spinal: isobaric asleep, lateral, metaraminol attached; midazolam 1 mg comforting

  • GA tips: sparing propofol (~60 mg); low threshold for ETT; bite-block; bladder temp

  • Long spinal (general): 3 ml 0.5% plain + 100 mcg morphine

Strongly consider TIVA to lower post-op delirium. If pre-op delirious — sevo + haloperidol.

Total hip replacement (THR)

  • GA LMA/ETT ± art line.

  • PENG + LCFN block.

  • Option: lateral asleep spinal with isobaric bupivacaine.

  • Consider TIVA (lower delirium).

  • Tranexamic acid 1 g at induction.

Total knee replacement (TKR)

  • GA LMA/ETT ± spinal.

  • Adductor canal block + IPACK (preserves motor).

  • Surgeon LIA; TXA 1 g.

  • Post-op: paracetamol + NSAID + oxycodone/morphine PRN.

Tibia ORIF — spinal

  • 3 ml 0.5% plain bupivacaine + 20 mcg fentanyl.

  • Plain = lateral bad-side-up, more comfortable.

Day-case orthopaedic spinals (prilocaine 2% plain)

Procedure Duration Dose 2% (ml)

Autograft ACL reconstruction 45–60 min 2.5
Bunionectomy 30–60 min 2.5
Major joint arthroplasty (THR/TKR) 60–90 min 2.5–3.0

4 · General / Breast

Diagnostic laparoscopy / I&D abscess (CEPOD)

  • Pre-op: paracetamol PO + NSAID (if not contraindicated)

  • Induction: 20G cannula; request 100 mcg fentanyl + 1 mg alfentanil; 75 mcg fentanyl at sign-in

  • Maintenance: propofol TCI 8→5 mcg/ml after LMA, or sevo (high MAC if robust)

  • Pre-KTS: alfentanil 250–500 mcg bolus 30 s before knife-to-skin

  • Antiemetic: dex 6.6 mg + ondansetron 4 mg

  • End: final 25 mcg fentanyl for recovery

Day-case lap cholecystectomy

  • Pre: paracetamol + NSAID

  • Peri: propofol/remi TCI (or sevo + fentanyl ± oxycodone)

  • TCI start: remi first to 6 ng/ml → wait 2 min → propofol 6 mcg/ml

  • Airway: COETT; atracurium; 16G cannula; depth monitor

  • End: fentanyl through case if sevo; end-of-case fentanyl if TIVA

  • Post: paracetamol + ibuprofen + tramadol/codeine; fentanyl 0–200 mcg in recovery

Laparoscopic appendicectomy

  • Modified RSI if indicated. 20G induction → 16G asleep.

  • Fentanyl ~200 mcg at induction; total ~300 mcg by end.

  • Propofol 2–3 mg/kg; rocuronium 1.2 mg/kg; COETT; sevo.

  • Diclofenac/IV ibuprofen; dex 6.6 mg; ondansetron 4 mg towards end.

  • Local to ports; reversal.

  • Complex appendix: MgSO₄ 50 mg/kg; morphine 0.1–0.2 mg/kg.

  • Post: paracetamol, NSAID, fentanyl 0–200 mcg (or alfentanil infusion if staying), oramorph 10–30 mg 2-hourly PRN.

Inguinal hernia repair

  • LMA unless specific reason for ETT.

  • Fentanyl + morphine; NSAID + paracetamol; antiemetics; antibiotics.

  • Block: TAP or ilio-inguinal field block.

DIEP — reconstructive breast surgery

  • 18G cannula; dex 6.6 mg + ondansetron 4–8 mg.

  • Propofol/remi TIVA; COETT.

  • Magnesium 50 mg/kg; TXA 1 g.

  • Paracetamol; oxycodone 10 mg.

  • Keep IV fluids 1.5–2 L max intra-op (flap physiology).

  • Surgeon-placed TAP blocks.

  • Post: paracetamol, oxycodone IR 5–10 mg 2-hourly; flap obs.

Long laparotomy / anterior resection / complex gynae

Set-up

  • Pre-induction 20G cannula; consider pre-med; check G&S.

  • Consent spinal, lines, HDU, and epidural vs rectus sheath catheters if open.

Awake spinal options

  • Option 1: 2 ml plain levobupivacaine 0.25–0.5% + 300 mcg diamorphine.

  • Option 2: 2 ml plain levobupivacaine 0.25–0.5% + 100–200 mcg PF morphine + 15–40 mcg fentanyl (aim 3 ml total).

Post-induction

  • 18G/16G cannula + arterial line; CO monitor (LiDCO / oesophageal Doppler).

  • Propofol/remi TIVA TCI; COETT.

  • Mg 50 mg/kg; TXA 1 g if bleeding; paracetamol; diclofenac if NSAID tolerant.

  • Diamorphine/oxycodone 0.1 mg/kg or morphine 0.2 mg/kg.

  • Epidural or rectus sheath catheters at end if open.

Thoracic epidural top-up

  • Test dose 0.25% levobupivacaine.

  • Then 10–15 ml 0.125% levobupivacaine every 40 min.

5 · ENT

Microlaryngoscopy (20-min day case)

  • Airway plan: subglottic jet ventilation catheter, MLT, injector needle, or cricothyroidotomy needle — decide at brief

  • Pre: paracetamol + NSAID; airway assessment — beware stridor

  • Peri: 20G cannula; propofol/remifentanil TCI; processed EEG; rocuronium 20–30 mg; ondansetron 4 mg + dex 6.6 mg

  • Catheter: tip to cords then insert ~8 cm further; repeat laryngoscopy + suction at end

  • Reversal: sugammadex often; remove jet catheter and mask-ventilate, or keep jetting until spontaneous

  • Post: fentanyl in recovery; TTO paracetamol + NSAID

MLT: traditional ventilation (not lasers), protects from blood/debris but impairs view. LA spray is comfortable but no drinking × 1 h.

Grommets (short paediatric ENT)

  • Pre: paracetamol + NSAID

  • Airway: LMA; distant airway; spontaneous ventilation

  • Opioid: alfentanil 1.5 mg bolus at induction; 0.5 mg increments as needed (anaesthetic.recipes)

  • Antiemetic: dexamethasone 6.6 mg

  • Maintenance: propofol TCI or sevoflurane

6 · Ophthalmic

Cataract surgery

  • 20–30 min; supine; pain +; blood loss nil.

  • Topical proxymetacaine, sub-Tenon's or peribulbar block — GA only if LA contraindicated.

  • Avoid heavy sedation (patients move suddenly).

  • O₂ 6 L/min under drape.

  • If GA: flexible LMA; deep anaesthesia (check iodine in eye + gaze); often no relaxant; ETT only if indicated.

  • Post: regular paracetamol; PRN antiemetic.

Peribulbar block

Injectate

  • 1:1 mix 2% lidocaine + 0.75% levobupivacaine with hyaluronidase 30 U/ml.

Technique

  • 25G × 25 mm peribulbar needle (bevel to globe).

  • Inferolateral: perpendicular through conjunctiva; if bone, withdraw 1 mm then redirect posteriorly under globe; depth 20–25 mm (hub at iris). Aspirate → 5–7 ml.

  • Medial (caruncle): slight medial angulation; feel 'click' through medial ligament. Depth 20–25 mm → 3–5 ml.

  • Massage eye to spread LA and normalise IOP (no Honan balloon).

Contra-indications

  • AL >26 mm (perforation risk); INR >1.5; infected/perforated eye; uncooperative patient.

Sub-Tenon's block

  • Landmark: sclerocorneal junction, inferonasal quadrant.

  • Proxymetacaine drops → iodine → speculum; patient looks up-and-out.

  • Lift conjunctiva (Moorfield's) → small incision (Westcott). Blunt dissect sclera from Tenon's.

  • Pass curved sub-Tenon cannula to hub; 3–5 ml of the same LA mix.

  • Much safer than peribulbar; can use with AL >26 mm.

  • Contra-indications: INR >2.5, infected/perforated eye, uncooperative.

Strabismus surgery

  • Paeds day case; OCR 50–80%; high PONV; higher MH incidence in some syndromes.

  • EMLA + explain parents + consent suppositories.

  • Atropine 15 mcg/kg IV on induction (prevent bradycardia).

  • LMA or ETT; ensure neutral gaze.

  • PR paracetamol 20 mg/kg + diclofenac 1 mg/kg.

  • Avoid sux (abnormal eye tone × 20 min); avoid opioids if possible; antiemetic.

  • Post: regular paracetamol, PRN diclofenac, topical amethocaine; cyclizine + ondansetron PRN.

Corneal graft (penetrating keratoplasty)

  • 90 min; open-eye — control IOP tightly.

  • GA LMA/ETT; avoid sux; NDMR with TOF.

  • Moderate hyperventilation + permissive hypotension.

  • Paracetamol + NSAID + opioid; antiemetic; anticholinergic PRN (OCR).

  • Acetazolamide 500 mg often used post-op.

Retinal detachment / vitrectomy

  • 90–120 min. Often elderly (IDDM/HTN/IHD).

  • Frequently LA ± sedation; may need top-up as it wears off.

  • If GA: secure airway (inaccessible intra-op); muscle relaxation to end; maintain IOP.

  • Avoid N₂O if SF₆ used.

  • Paracetamol + antiemetic; oramorph PRN post-op.

  • Positioning: may need face-down post-op (SF₆).

Neonatal anaesthesia pearls

  • TIVA models only go to ~5 kg — track ml/h, convert to true mg/kg/h.

  • Antibiotics: metronidazole 15 mg/kg; cefuroxime 7.5 mg/kg.

  • Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg; paracetamol 7.5 mg/kg.

  • Dilute fentanyl to 10 mcg/ml for titration.

  • Set ventilator BEFORE attaching; switch auxiliary → circle circuit.

  • Starting vent: TV 4–6 ml/kg (≈ 40–60 ml), RR 26, short I-time.

  • Ambient temp ↑; tube depth ≈ ETT size × 3 (e.g. size 3 cuffed ≈ 9–10 cm at lips).

  • BIS not licensed <1 y and neonatal modules often fail.

8a · Day-case spinal — prilocaine 2% plain

Single-shot, ambulation expected. Block resolves ~90–150 min. Onset ~5 min. Add fentanyl 10–20 mcg only if longer than 60 min or for visceral procedures.

Rules of thumb: pick fentanyl if you want a quicker, cleaner block for a day case; diamorphine for a half-day of analgesia after major joint, urology or short laparotomy; PF morphine if the patient is staying overnight and you need 24 h cover (LSCS, long open laparotomy). Don't combine PF morphine with diamorphine.

8c · Obstetric spinals

Elective LSCS (NALHN house recipe)

  • Mix: 2.2–2.4 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine + 15 mcg fentanyl + 100 mcg PF morphine

  • 2.2 ml for short stature; 2.4 ml standard. Target T4 (light touch), T6 (cold), sacral block confirmed.

  • Co-load 500–1000 ml crystalloid; metaraminol prophylactic infusion or boluses (phenylephrine where available).

  • Carbetocin 100 mcg slow IV in two pushes post-delivery; keep syringe separate.

Category-1 LSCS (rapid sequence spinal)

  • Mix: 2.4–2.6 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine + 15 mcg fentanyl ± 100 mcg PF morphine (omit if it would delay)

  • No skin local, no aspiration of CSF before injection, single attempt — escalate to GA if not in by ~3 min.

Cervical cerclage

  • Mix: 2.2 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine + 15 mcg fentanyl

  • Sit up 30–60 s; slight head-up after lying — target ≈ T10. Confirm sacral block (feet) — can be sacral-sparing.

Manual removal of placenta / ERPC after delivery

  • Mix: 2.0–2.2 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine + 15 mcg fentanyl — target T8–T10.

  • Active bleeding: prefer GA. Beware ongoing concealed loss before commiting to neuraxial.

8d · Orthopaedic / trauma spinals

#NOF · hemiarthroplasty · DHS · TFNA · Gamma nail (short list)

  • Mix: 3 ml 0.5% plain bupivacaine + 20 mcg fentanyl

  • Lateral bad-side-up (plain spreads upward in this position → denser block on operative side).

  • Metaraminol attached; midazolam 1 mg comforting. Bladder temp.

#NOF (longer cases, post-op morphine sparing)

  • Mix: 3 ml 0.5% plain bupivacaine + 100 mcg PF morphine

  • Provides 18–24 h analgesia; admit to ward bed with respiratory observations.

Total hip replacement (elective, lateral asleep spinal)

  • Mix: 3 ml 0.5% isobaric (or plain) bupivacaine + 15–20 mcg fentanyl ± 100 mcg PF morphine

  • Asleep, lateral with operative side up; metaraminol attached before turning. Pair with PENG + LCFN block.

Total knee replacement

  • Mix: 2.5–3.0 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine + 15 mcg fentanyl

  • Add 100 mcg PF morphine for staying-overnight patients without LIA; pair with adductor canal + IPACK.

Tibia / ankle ORIF

  • Mix: 3 ml 0.5% plain bupivacaine + 20 mcg fentanyl, lateral bad-side-up

  • Or hyperbaric 2.5–3.0 ml supine + 15 mcg fentanyl — sit briefly if very tall.

Lower-limb amputation (BKA / AKA)

  • Mix: 2.5–3 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine + 15–20 mcg fentanyl + 100 mcg PF morphine

  • Target T10. Treat coexisting peripheral vascular disease + diabetes — metaraminol low-threshold, careful with hypotension.

8e · Urology spinals

TURP · TURBT · bladder neck incision (regular elective)

  • Mix: 2.5–3 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine + 15 mcg fentanyl

  • Target T10 — covers bladder distension and obturator stimulation. Awake spinal helpful for early detection of TUR syndrome and bladder perforation.

Optical urethrotomy / cystoscopy ± stent

  • Mix: 2.0–2.5 ml prilocaine 2% plain (day-case, see table) or 2 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine if longer.

Hydrocele / scrotal / testicular surgery

  • Mix: 2.5–3.0 ml prilocaine 2% plain (day-case) or 2.5 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine + 15 mcg fentanyl (longer cases).

Robotic cystectomy (spinal as analgesic adjunct or sole anaesthetic if combined with GA)

  • Mix: 2 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine + 500 mcg diamorphine

  • Run with TIVA + relaxant for the GA component; consider throat pack — head-down gastric pooling risk.

Robotic nephrectomy

  • Mix: 2 ml 0.5% hyperbaric L-bupivacaine + 500 mcg diamorphine (total ~2.5 ml)

  • Useful when incision is large; reduces post-op opioid requirement.

Robot-assisted radical prostatectomy (RALP)

  • Mix: 1.5 ml 0.5% hyperbaric L-bupivacaine + 700 mcg diamorphine (total ~2.2 ml)

  • Higher diamorphine for substantial post-op pain; pair with dex 6.6 mg + ondansetron 4 mg.

8f · General / colorectal spinals

Open inguinal / femoral hernia repair

  • Mix: 2.5–3.0 ml prilocaine 2% plain (day-case) or 2.5–3.0 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine + 15 mcg fentanyl (longer/recurrent)

  • Target T8–T10; cover for peritoneal traction.

Lap chole / lap appendix (rarely spinal-only; usually GA)

  • Generally GA with regional adjunct. Spinal anaesthesia for laparoscopy is a specialist technique — needs T4 block, IV opioid + antiemetic and shoulder-tip preparation.

Long laparotomy / anterior resection / pelvic exenteration (analgesic spinal alongside GA)

  • Option 1: 2 ml plain levobupivacaine 0.25–0.5% + 300 mcg diamorphine.

  • Option 2: 2 ml plain levobupivacaine 0.25–0.5% + 100–200 mcg PF morphine + 15–40 mcg fentanyl (aim 3 ml total).

  • Place before induction (awake spinal), then proceed with GA; rectus sheath catheters or epidural at end if open.

8g · Vascular spinals

Femoro-popliteal bypass / lower-limb angioplasty / embolectomy

  • Mix: 2.5–3 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine + 15 mcg fentanyl

  • Target T10. Continue antiplatelets per local protocol; check coags + recent heparin timing before siting.

AV fistula / brachio-cephalic (usually regional)

  • Spinal rarely used — preferred axillary/supraclavicular block ± sedation; spinal not an option for upper-limb work.

EVAR (selected low-risk cases)

  • Mix: 2.5–3 ml 0.5% hyperbaric bupivacaine + 15 mcg fentanyl (target T8)

  • CSE or LA infiltration commonly preferred — discuss with surgeon re duration / patient tolerance.

8h · Saddle & perineal blocks

Target S2–S5. Sit patient up 5–10 min after injecting hyperbaric solution to confine to sacral roots; injectate stays low because of glucose loading.

8i · Practical tips

Choosing the LA

  • Prilocaine 2% plain — day-case workhorse; resolves <150 min; minimal urinary retention. Avoid if PMH G6PD or in pregnancy.

  • Hyperbaric (heavy) bupivacaine 0.5% — predictable, dense block; controlled by posture; default for LSCS, urology, ortho overnight.

  • Plain (isobaric) bupivacaine 0.5% — denser on dependent side after injection; classic for trauma lateral bad-side-up; less hypotension than heavy in elderly.

  • Plain levobupivacaine 0.25–0.5% — for analgesic spinal alongside GA in long laparotomy.

Posture & block height

  • Saddle: sit up 5–10 min with heavy LA.

  • T10 (TURP, hernia, lower limb): supine after heavy LA; minor head-down if not climbing.

  • T4 (LSCS, upper abdo): supine then 5–10° head-down ~3 min if needed; lateral tilt for obstetric aortocaval.

Vasopressor & co-load

  • Metaraminol attached before intrathecal injection in elderly, obstetric, cardiac and any case targeting >T6.

  • Co-load 5–10 ml/kg crystalloid at the time of injection (not pre-load — wears off).

When to convert / abandon

  • Block not rising after 15 min from full dose → do not redose; switch plan.

  • Sacral sparing despite adequate volume → reposition (prone-knee-chest 2 min) or top up via CSE; otherwise GA.

  • Patient distress mid-procedure → IV fentanyl 25–50 mcg; ketamine 20 mg; convert to GA early rather than late.

Safety

  • Two-person check of any intrathecal opioid; label syringe with drug + dose; second syringe for the LA only.

  • Document block level + sacral block before knife-to-skin.

  • Follow-up next day for any patient who received intrathecal morphine or diamorphine — late respiratory depression, retention, pruritus.

9 · Non-theatre & scopes

ECT

Key considerations

  • Unprotected airway; remote location; sympathetic/parasympathetic surge; ↑ IOP/IGP/ICP; comorbidities; psychotropics.

Contra-indications

  • Absolute: MI <3 months, CVA <1 month, phaeochromocytoma

  • Relative: ↑ ICP, severe cardiac disease, aortic/cerebral aneurysm, sux apnoea / MH (use NDMR)

Technique

  • Fast list; confirm no new medical issues; previous prop/sux dose noted.

  • IV access; NIBP, sats, face-mask pre-oxygenate.

  • Propofol = previous dose − 20–30 mg; WAIT; top-up to LOV.

  • Sux (same dose as before); flush IV.

  • Support airway; hyperventilate (good for seizure induction).

  • ECT when fasciculations stop.

  • Support airway ± OPA post-stim; Hudson on and leave room if stable, SV.

Rescue

  • Hypertensive → esmolol 20–30 mg (2–3 ml of 10 mg/ml).

  • Bradycardic → atropine.

Endoscopy / UGI / ERCP / Colonoscopy

Decide: sedation vs ETT ± RSI

  • Aspiration risk, shared airway, hypovolaemia.

  • If RSI: sit up, OptiFlow, VL requested, suction under pillow, good drip, sux (short-acting).

Sedation

  • Propofol hand boluses 30–60 mg then small titrated boluses.

  • ± fentanyl 25–100 mcg.

  • Alternative: propofol TCI 2–6 mcg/ml Ce.

  • OptiFlow for UGI; capnography imperfect — watch the patient.

Colonoscopy

  • As for sedation; Hudson + capnography usually fine.

ERCP

  • Longer, prone/semi-prone, aspiration risk — favour GA ETT with RSI.
